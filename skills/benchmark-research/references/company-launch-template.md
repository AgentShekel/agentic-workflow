# Research Brief — [Company] Playbook

## Company

[Company]

## Why this company is in the benchmark

- [Growth velocity / category relevance / unusual mechanism]

## Goal

Produce a deep reverse-engineering analysis of [Company]'s growth
playbook based on the source archive.

## Deliverables

- `[company]-playbook-analysis.md`
- `[company]-playbook-slides-outline.md`

## Focus areas

- wedge
- target buyer and user
- sales cycle
- pilot / POC mechanics
- pricing and economics
- implementation model
- trust / compliance / integration mechanics
- expansion logic
- why the company won
- what is transferable to similar companies
- what is not transferable

## Evidence rule

Every important claim must be sourced or explicitly labelled as
inference / unverified / open question — use `[E] [I] [UV] [OQ] [H]`.

## Final decision questions

- What is the real growth machine?
- What was the initial wedge?
- What commercial logic made the model work?
- What part of the playbook can other companies copy?
- What part depends on unique timing, founder credibility, market
  structure, or capital environment?
